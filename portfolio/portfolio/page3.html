Macbeth's Ambition Essay

The legendary play of Macbeth by playwright, Shakespeare, takes a stance on the costs of ambition as shown through the eyes of the Thane of Cawdor, Macbeth. The play and essay itself to warn its audience of selfish ambition with both reference to both the text as well as real life with famous figures such as Juluis Caesar, Napoleon, or even the most infamous man of the 20th century, Adolf Hitler. Overall the essay uses both in tandum to build on the point of what unbridled ambition can cause and when it is no longer ambition but obsession showing the true significance of what the message Shakespeare trys to convey through his play.
